# Knowledge Base: Irish Construction Cost Decomposition

## Established Facts

1. **Hard costs = 53% of total delivery cost nationally** (SCSI); 49% in Dublin (higher land share). Materials ~25%, labour ~25%, site works ~3%.
2. **All-Materials CAGR 2015-2024 = 3.99%/yr**, reaching index 147.5 from base 100.
3. **Labour cost CAGR = 4.03%/yr** (construction hourly total labour cost, EHQ03). Materials and labour grew at essentially identical rates over the full decade.
4. **PC1 explains 82.4% of material price variance**, and 3 components capture 90%. Material prices are driven by a small number of latent factors.
5. **Structural breaks at COVID (Mar 2020) and Ukraine (Feb 2022)** are both highly significant (p < 0.001 via Chow test).
6. **Employment doubled (+103%) while output volume grew only 9%** between 2015 and latest data. Apparent productivity decline or compositional shift.
7. **Tender price inflation = 3.0% for 2024** (SCSI), down from 6.2% in 2023 and ~14% in 2021-2022.
8. **NZEB cost uplift was officially estimated at 1.9%** (SEAI 2019) but our pre/post analysis shows excess inflation of 2.3-5.1pp/yr in affected materials.

## What Works

- **CAGR ranking** identifies the fastest-growing materials but misses share-weighting
- **Variance decomposition** identifies which materials DRIVE the aggregate index
- **PCA** confirms low-dimensional factor structure
- **Weighted contribution (share x growth)** is the most policy-relevant metric
- **Structural break detection** confirms regime changes at known shock dates

## What Doesn't Work / Pitfalls

- **Aggregate indices mask enormous heterogeneity**: All-Materials grew 4%/yr but individual materials range from 0.5% to 11.4%
- **Pre/post NZEB comparison is confounded** by COVID/Ukraine occurring shortly after NZEB implementation
- **Employment-output gap** may reflect measurement issues in the volume index rather than a real productivity decline
- **Buildcost.ie trade breakdown** is approximate — not a measured decomposition but a professional estimate range
- **Material weights in the All-Materials index** are not published by the CSO, making true weighted decomposition impossible without additional assumptions

## Surprises

1. **Glass barely moved** (CAGR 0.48%) despite NZEB triple-glazing mandate — suggests supply response was adequate
2. **Timber showed the largest COVID spike (+70%)** but has since mean-reverted to -21% below peak
3. **Cement ratcheted upward** through both crises without reverting — structural demand floor from housing targets
4. **Electrical fittings** showed the largest excess post-NZEB inflation (+5.1pp/yr) — heat pump/solar demand shock
5. **Materials and labour grew at near-identical rates** (3.99% vs 4.03%) — the narrative that "materials are the problem" is not supported by the data over the full decade
