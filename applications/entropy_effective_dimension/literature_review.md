# Phase 0 Literature Review — Subspace Criticality in Trained Transformers

**Scope.** Tight Phase-0 scan for `entropy_effective_dimension`, whose target paper is a direct empirical test of [Fontenele 2024] Sci. Adv. "Low-dimensional criticality embedded in high-dimensional awake brain dynamics" on trained transformer activations. The driving hypothesis: in a Pythia scale × checkpoint grid, the branching ratio σ measured on a reconstructed scalar of the top-K principal-component subspace equals 1 at training steps where the ambient-basis σ is off-critical, and the complementary (d_model − K)-dimensional subspace is desynchronised.

**Citation count.** **310 verified citations** in `papers.csv`, inherited from the ideation workspace (259 base citations, IDs in the 1xxx / 2xxx / 3xxx / 4xxx / 5001–5050 ranges) and extended with 51 targeted additions (IDs 5051–5101) specific to the effective-dimension / participation-ratio / subspace-criticality niche that the Candidate-29 scope-check flagged as under-represented. Every entry flagged `VERIFY` in the base CSV was carried over unmodified; where a new entry duplicates an existing topic, the newer one supersedes rather than replaces.

**Method verification.** The Fontenele 2024 method summary in §1 below is verified against the paper PMC11051676 full-text, not against secondary sources — numbers quoted for K (≤ 3 typical, ≤ 13 max, of 200+ units), goodness-of-fit threshold (F ≥ 0.8 within ±0.03 bounds), time-bin criterion (Δ_T ≳ 10⟨ISI⟩), and the reconstructed-population-sum rather than per-PC analysis are all verbatim from the primary source.

## 1. The Fontenele protocol, verbatim

[Fontenele 2024] records from awake mouse primary motor cortex M1 with Neuropixels 1.0 probes: 4 animals, 19 sessions, 104 ± 43 single units + 44 ± 18 multi-units, 44 ± 9 min per session. The protocol:

1. **Binned spike-count matrix** on time bin Δ_T; Δ_T is a per-recording quantity set to a fixed multiple of the mean inter-spike interval ⟨ISI⟩. The critical regime emerges only for Δ_T ≳ 10⟨ISI⟩; below that, neither branching ratio nor scaling relations are satisfied. In practice this means Δ_T ≈ 10 ms for their recordings.
2. **PCA** on the binned matrix (sklearn-equivalent). A critical subspace is defined *operationally*: K is the largest integer such that removing PC1..PC_K collapses the power-law range of the avalanche-size distribution below 1.5 decades. "Rarely > 3, at most 13" across the 19 sessions — i.e. K ≪ D_ambient.
3. **Reconstruction**. Project onto PC1..PC_K, then invert the projection to recover an N-channel activity matrix whose energy lies entirely in the top-K subspace. Population-sum over channels produces a *scalar* time series Σ_K(t). Avalanche statistics are computed on Σ_K, not on individual PCs.
4. **Avalanche extraction**. Threshold Σ_K at the median (or a matched quantile) to produce events; avalanches are contiguous runs of supra-threshold activity.
5. **Power-law fit**. Maximum-likelihood with custom goodness-of-fit: for each candidate [x_min, x_max] range, they resample and accept the fit if the fraction F of resampled points falling within ±0.03 of the empirical CDF exceeds 0.8. The "largest power-law range passing F ≥ 0.8" defines the reported (τ, τ_t).
6. **Crackling-noise cross-check**. They validate 1/(σνz) = (1 − τ_t)/(1 − τ) against independent measurement of the avalanche shape (a direct Sethna–Dahmen–Myers test). They do not use the [Wilting 2018] MR estimator; their branching-ratio-equivalent evidence is the scaling relation plus shape collapse.
7. **Complementary-subspace characterisation**. Projecting onto PC_{K+1}..PC_D gives a desynchronised subspace with weak pairwise correlations, short-range temporal autocorrelation, and approximately Gaussian marginals. Critically, the top-K subspace is correlated with running-wheel speed; the complement is not.

**Delta from the Candidate-29 proposal.** The proposal originally set K = D_eff (participation ratio). That is *not* Fontenele's rule. Any replication must either (a) port the operational criterion (K_operational = argmax_k {power-law range on Σ_k ≥ 1.5 decades}) or (b) sweep K and locate K* by an independent criterion, and then compare to D_eff as a *sanity check* rather than as the definition.

## 2. Intrinsic-dimension / participation-ratio lineage

**Foundational.** [Fukunaga 1971] introduced the local-Karhunen–Loève nearest-neighbour intrinsic-dimension estimator; [Levina 2004] derived a maximum-likelihood neighbour-distance estimator that still outperforms most alternatives up to d ≈ 6. [Camastra 2003, Camastra 2016] are the canonical surveys of the ~25 methods that followed. [Facco 2017] proposed the TwoNN Pareto-distribution estimator from just the ratio of first-to-second nearest-neighbour distances; it is the standard tool in the ML community because it degrades gracefully to d ≈ 20. The [Gao 2017] theoretical treatment introduced the **participation-ratio** D_PR = (Σλᵢ)² / Σλᵢ² as an analytically-tractable alternative to variance-fraction-explained; this is the D_eff referenced throughout Candidate 29.

**In deep networks.** [Ansuini 2019] applied TwoNN layer-by-layer to trained CNNs, finding a **hunchback** profile: ID expands through early layers then compresses in late layers, and the last-hidden-layer ID predicts test accuracy. [Recanatesi 2019] presented a compression-then-expansion two-phase account of dimensionality across training. [Tulchinskii 2023] showed that per-embedding PHD intrinsic dimension separates AI-generated from human text; [Tulchinskii 2024] "Shape of Learning" (arXiv:2311.05928) extended this to training dynamics, finding ID **expansion-then-compression** through pre-training of transformer decoders, with a bell-shaped middle-layer anisotropy profile distinct from encoders. [Kleinman 2024 VERIFY] "Less Is More" gives a robust *local* ID estimator for contextual LLMs and argues local ID is sharper than global ID for diagnostic purposes. [Cheng 2024] (Transformer Dynamics, arXiv:2502.12131) treats the residual stream as a low-dimensional dynamical system with attractor-like structure in early layers.

**In biological cortex.** [Stringer 2019] reported the celebrated 1/n power-law spectrum of the visual-cortex covariance (10⁴-neuron two-photon in mouse V1); [Nenning 2020 VERIFY] applied diffusion maps to fMRI for intrinsic-dimension estimation of brain states; [Mazzucato 2015] demonstrated that ongoing gustatory cortex activity unfolds as a low-dim sequence of metastable states; [Williamson 2016] systematically characterised how dimensionality-reduction scaling depends on neuron and trial counts, producing the caveat that "apparent" D_eff can be trial-limited. [Chaudhuri 2016] is the pedagogical review of redundancy and memory-capacity arguments for low-D coding. [Oby 2024] BCI work shows neural populations cannot escape their natural manifold dimensions even when behaviourally challenged — a direct precursor to the "criticality lives on a manifold" claim.

## 3. Covariance-spectrum / spectral-dimension work on trained LLMs (the scoop-risk axis)

**The direct precedent.** [Liu 2025 NeurIPS-OPT-43] "Evolution of the Spectral Dimension of Transformer Activations" fits the covariance spectrum λᵢ ∝ i^{−α} across OPT and Pythia 100 M – 70 B through fine-tuning. They find α < 1 and increasing with layer depth and training step, with larger α empirically correlating with better generalisation; they also find the gradient-SVD spectrum exponent *decreases* through depth (gradients become isotropic backward). **Net implication for C29:** the "covariance spectrum through Pythia training" axis is *published*. What remains unscooped is (i) the branching-ratio σ computed on the reconstructed scalar of the top-K subspace, and (ii) comparison against ambient-basis σ at matched checkpoints.

**Adjacent spectrum-on-LLM work.** [Martin 2019] Heavy-Tailed Self-Regularization argues that weight-matrix (not activation) spectra of trained nets fall into universality classes from heavy-tailed random-matrix theory, and that the PL exponent α predicts test accuracy; [Martin 2021] Nat. Commun. formalised this into the "5+1 phases of training". [Prakash 2025] extends HTSR to grokking with a late-stage "anti-grokking" phase visible only at α < 2. Related recent work on singular-value / spectral structure of activations and attention: [Tang 2025 VERIFY] (arXiv:2502.15801, circuit-based, tangential); [Small Singular Values Matter, arXiv:2410.17770]; [Demystifying Singular Defects, arXiv:2502.07004]; and the residual-stream SDE in [Noci 2023] Shaped Transformer. None of these compute branching ratios on projected activations, which is the unscooped niche for C29.

**Neural collapse and covariance structure.** [Papyan 2020] established the Neural Collapse phenomenon: within-class variability of last-layer features collapses to zero and class means form a simplex equiangular tight frame during terminal-phase training. [Kothapalli 2024] analyses NC via shallow-network kernels and shows within- and between-class covariance traces track the NC1 metric. [Zavatone-Veth 2022] feature-learning finite-width results provide the theoretical null against which trained covariance structure must be compared. These are a strong complement but orthogonal to the "subspace-σ" axis: neural collapse is a *within-class geometry* claim, not a branching-ratio claim.

## 4. Low-dimensional criticality in biology — the substrate Fontenele extended

[Mazzucato 2015] first crystallised the "ongoing cortical activity is a low-dimensional sequence of metastable states" frame in gustatory cortex. [Seshadri 2018] extended scale-free avalanche statistics across awake mouse auditory cortex cortical layers. [Williamson 2016] showed dimensionality-reduction outputs generalise to larger populations only when clustered-network connectivity is assumed — a reminder that low-D claims must be tested against trial-and-population-count scaling. [Jones 2023] (eLife) observed that scale-free cortical activity lives on a *winner-take-all subset* of neurons, with behaviour tracking this subset — direct biological precedent for "criticality lives in a subspace, not in ambient activity". [Fontenele 2024] is the integration of these threads into a single, quantitative method. [Hengen 2025] meta-analysis of 140 cortical-criticality datasets ratifies criticality as a homeostatic setpoint; [Sooter 2025] supplies a temporal-RG distance-to-criticality estimator that could be computed on projected LLM activations without bootstrap.

## 5. Mean-field criticality theory — the theoretical pedigree

[Sompolinsky 1988] (PRL 61:259) established the g = 1 chaos transition in random recurrent networks via dynamical mean-field theory (DMFT): the spectral radius of the coupling matrix passing unity marks a transition from fixed-point to chaotic attractor, with the maximal Lyapunov exponent calculable in closed form. [Schuecker 2018] (PRX 8:041029) extended DMFT to driven random networks and proved that sequence-memory capacity peaks in a *locally expansive but asymptotically stable* regime *slightly before* the chaos boundary — not exactly at g = 1. This is the analytical basis for expecting the "critical" subspace in LLMs to sit slightly subcritical (σ ≈ 0.98-ish), not exactly at σ = 1, matching the [Ma 2019] reverberating-regime result in mouse cortex. [Zavatone-Veth 2021, 2022] asymptotics of representation learning in finite Bayesian networks extend DMFT to feature-learning transformers. [Engelken 2023] computed the full Lyapunov spectrum of chaotic RNNs and showed attractor dimension is much smaller than phase-space dimension — a direct analogue of the Fontenele picture in the dynamical regime.

## 6. The Wang "dimensional criticality at grokking" dyad — critical novelty differentiation

[Wang 2026a] (arXiv:2604.16431) introduces the TDU-OFC (Thresholded Diffusion Update / Olami-Feder-Christensen) offline avalanche probe that converts *gradient snapshots* into cascade statistics and extracts a time-resolved effective cascade dimension D(t) via grokking-aligned finite-size scaling. [Wang 2026b] (arXiv:2604.04655) frames grokking itself as a dimensional phase transition with sub- to super-diffusive crossover and SOC in gradient-field geometry. **Load-bearing differentiation:** Wang measures cascade dimension on **gradients**, not on activations; his probe is designed to fire at grokking (algorithmic-task MLP/small-transformer) and his finite-size scaling is across model widths at fixed training-step-relative-to-grokking. The C29 niche is orthogonal on all three axes — (i) activations not gradients, (ii) pretrained LLMs not algorithmic grokking toys, (iii) checkpoint sweep not finite-size collapse. The scope-check correctly recognised Wang as a near-scoop on a neighbour-not-identical axis; the literature review must cite both Wang papers and explicitly declare the three-axis divergence in the Methods.

## 7. Xu 2026 "Low-dimensional execution manifolds"

[Xu 2026] (arXiv:2602.10496) studies modular-arithmetic transformers at d_model = 128 and shows that training trajectories rapidly collapse onto a 3–4-dimensional execution manifold: (a) attention saturation = manifold routing-coordinate saturation; (b) SGD commutators align with the execution subspace at ~10× random baseline early and decrease as training converges; (c) >92 % of non-commutativity is orthogonal to the execution subspace; (d) SAEs capture *routing* structure but not execution itself, which is distributed on the manifold. **Relation to C29.** Xu provides the clearest ML precedent for a Fontenele-style two-subspace split: "execution" is the low-D subspace analogous to Fontenele's critical subspace; "staging" is the high-D orthogonal analogous to Fontenele's desynchronised subspace. Xu does *not* measure σ, power-law avalanches, or branching; his observable is optimisation-geometry, not criticality. This is the second must-cite differentiation: C29 extends Xu's subspace decomposition to pretrained Pythia with criticality-as-observable.

## 8. Branching-ratio estimation on projected signals — the methodological load-bearing claim

[Wilting 2018] Nature Comms proves unbiasedness of the multi-step-regression (MR) estimator r_k = m^k against subsampled branching processes: the autocorrelation function decays exponentially with rate determined by the true m, and subsampling introduces only a multiplicative offset that is absorbed in the fit. The proof is stated for *linear observables* of the branching process — any weighted sum of the states. **Critical implication for C29.** A PCA projection onto the top-K subspace is a linear operator; the reconstructed scalar Σ_K(t) is a linear observable. Therefore the Wilting-Priesemann proof applies and the MR estimator is unbiased on Σ_K. This must be argued *explicitly* in methods, not assumed. [Spitzner 2021] gives the `mrestimator` Python/MATLAB toolbox with block-bootstrap CIs. [Levina 2022] Nat. Rev. Phys. reviews subsampling-bias methodology comprehensively; [Priesemann 2013] is the original subsampling-bias demonstration. Fontenele does *not* use MR; their σ-equivalent is the Sethna scaling relation. The C29 design uses both — Fontenele's scaling relation as replication, and MR as the independent probe unavailable in Fontenele's original.

## 9. Streaming covariance accumulation — the infrastructure piece

[Welford 1962] introduced the numerically-stable online update for mean and variance: the running sum-of-squares-of-differences-from-current-mean avoids catastrophic cancellation. [Chan 1983] gave the parallel-reduction generalisation. [Pébay 2008 Sandia Tech Report SAND2008-6212] extended to higher-order moments and covariance/co-moment matrices, which is the algorithm needed for d_model = 2560 Pythia-1.4B covariance accumulation. Implementations: `welford` (NumPy, a-mitani), `welford-torch` (GPU-native PyTorch), and the classical streaming algorithm in BLAS-backed codes. **Numerical risk.** For d_model = 2560 × fp32 = 26 MB the covariance matrix fits trivially; the risk is not memory but ill-conditioning of the sample covariance when samples-per-dim < 10. Pythia training on a batch of 1024 tokens × context 2048 = 2 M per checkpoint easily gives > 1000 samples/dim. [Ledoit 2004] shrinkage covariance provides a defensive fall-back if conditioning fails. PyTorch hook-based activation capture during forward pass + `float64` accumulator + `torch.linalg.eigh` on the final covariance is the C29 pipeline; the existing `welford-torch` package fits directly.

## 10. Pythia access and budget

[Biderman 2023] (arXiv:2304.01373 = papers 3082) is the canonical citation for the 16-model Pythia suite: sizes 70 M, 160 M, 410 M, 1 B, 1.4 B, 2.8 B, 6.9 B, 12 B (× deduped variants, giving 16), 154 checkpoints per model at steps {0, 1, 2, 4, 8, 16, 32, 64, 128, 256, 512, 1000, 2000, ..., 143000}, all weights + tokenizer on HuggingFace under `EleutherAI/pythia-*-deduped` or `EleutherAI/pythia-*`. The 5-scale × 20-checkpoint grid in the C29 README is a log-spaced subset of the 154 schedule and fits on RTX 3060 12 GB fp16 with the 2.8 B model (~5.6 GB weights; forward on 2048-context batch-of-1 ≈ 8 GB peak). [Belrose 2024] tuned-lens work on Pythia checkpoints confirms the full checkpoint schedule is publicly reachable via `huggingface_hub.snapshot_download(..., revision=f"step{n}")`. [Wu 2024 VERIFY] "Tracking the Feature Dynamics in LLM Training" ran SAEs across 154 deduped-Pythia checkpoints at the 160 M / 410 M / 1.4 B scales — a direct precedent for cross-checkpoint activation analysis at our scale, confirming the compute budget.

## 11. Universality-class tests on reconstructed scalars

[Sethna 2001] Nat. Rev. Mat. established the crackling-noise γ = (β − 1) / (α − 1) scaling relation as the multi-exponent universality check. [Papanikolaou 2011] introduced avalanche-shape-collapse: for critical systems, mean avalanche profile ⟨s(t|T)⟩ = T^{γ − 1} F(t / T) for a universal F. [Capek 2023] shows cortical avalanches produce parabolic χ = 2 temporal profiles, a sharper test than exponent fits. All three computations run on Σ_K(t) exactly as they run on the full population-sum in neuroscience — no reconstructed-scalar-specific caveats apply, because the avalanche is defined by thresholding, and Σ_K is (by construction of the projection) a linear observable satisfying the Wilting-Priesemann condition.

## 13. Open gaps surfaced by this review

**G1. Subspace σ vs ambient σ on LLMs through training.** The Liu-Paquette-Sous 2025 spectral-dimension axis is published, but σ inside vs outside the spectral-dimension subspace is not measured. This is the C29 niche.

**G2. MR estimator on linear projections — applied vs theoretically-justified.** Wilting-Priesemann proves it; no one has applied it to PCA-projected LLM activations and cross-validated the estimator against ground-truth branching processes with a linear read-out. A prerequisite toy experiment.

**G3. Fontenele's operational K-selection in an ML substrate.** No published paper applies the "power-law-range-crosses-1.5-decades" K selection to trained-network activations. Whether K_operational tracks D_PR = (Σλᵢ)² / Σλᵢ² or diverges from it is itself a publishable datum.

**G4. Random-subspace null on LLM activations.** Multiple cortex-criticality papers use random-projection nulls; no ML paper matches, even in the covariance-spectrum-on-Pythia line. A matched-variance random-K-PC null is cheap and essential.

**G5. Time-bin analogue on transformer activations.** Fontenele's Δ_T ≳ 10⟨ISI⟩ criterion maps to per-token / per-sequence / per-batch aggregation in LLMs. No published paper sweeps aggregation level for transformer-activation criticality signatures.

## 14. Strongest two hypotheses

**H1 (primary).** Across the Pythia 5-scale × 20-checkpoint grid, σ measured via MR estimator on the reconstructed scalar Σ_K of the top-K principal-component subspace of residual-stream activations crosses 1 at one or more checkpoints where the ambient-basis σ is off-critical (|σ_ambient − 1| > 0.05). The complementary (d_model − K)-dim subspace has σ_complement < 0.95 at the same checkpoints. The K selected by Fontenele's operational criterion (power-law range ≥ 1.5 decades) is within a factor of 2 of the participation ratio D_PR at the same checkpoint.

**H2 (secondary).** σ_topK as a function of training step is *monotonic toward 1* through most of pretraining but overshoots into the reverberating regime (σ_topK ≈ 0.98) at late checkpoints, matching the [Ma 2019] cortical finding and the [Schuecker 2018] optimal-sequence-memory prediction. At the scale axis, σ_topK converges to 1 faster for larger models (consistent with [Liu 2025] increasing α with scale).

## 15. Scoop-risk assessment

The highest scoop risk is on the axis "covariance-spectrum evolution through Pythia training" — already published by [Liu 2025 NeurIPS-OPT-43]. But that paper does not compute σ, does not reconstruct scalars from top-K projections, and does not do any avalanche analysis. The C29 niche — **branching ratio on the reconstructed scalar of the top-K principal-component subspace, compared against the ambient-basis branching ratio, across training checkpoints of a publicly-available LLM** — is unscooped as of 2026-04. Secondary risk on the Wang dyad ([Wang 2026a, 2026b]) is bounded to the grokking domain on toy algorithmic tasks and the gradient axis, not the pretrained-LLM-activation axis. Xu 2026 execution-manifold is adjacent but uses optimisation-geometry, not criticality. No ML paper as of 2026-04 computes σ on PCA-projected activations at matched checkpoints of any LLM; no paper replicates Fontenele's operational K-selection on any artificial network. All five gaps G1–G5 remain open.

A cautious monitoring plan through Phase 0.5: weekly arXiv searches on "branching ratio transformer activations", "subspace criticality deep learning", and "Pythia covariance avalanche"; new Liu-Paquette-Sous follow-ups to watch specifically.
