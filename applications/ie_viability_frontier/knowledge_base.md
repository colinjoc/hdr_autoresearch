# Knowledge Base — Irish Residential Development Viability

## Established Facts

1. **National median new house price (end 2025): ~€387,000** (CSO HPM09, PPR data). Dublin ~€480k, Cork ~€340k, rural counties €160-235k.
2. **Construction cost per sqm (Buildcost H1 2025): €2,428-3,020/sqm** depending on region and house type. National average for 4-bed semi: ~€2,690/sqm.
3. **Average new house size: ~110 sqm.** Construction cost per unit: ~€266k-332k.
4. **Land cost: CSO RZLPA02 median varies €156k-€1,588k per hectare** by county. At 40 units/ha: €3,900-39,700 per unit.
5. **Development contributions: €8k-28k per unit** depending on local authority.
6. **Part V obligation: 20% of units in schemes of 10+** transferred at below-market price since 2021.
7. **Finance rate for development: ~7-12% per annum**, 60-70% LTC typical.
8. **Build duration: ~2-3 years** from commencement to completion.
9. **Developer profit margin: 15-20% of GDV** required for development to proceed.
10. **RZLT rate: 3% per annum** on market value of zoned undeveloped land from 2025.
11. **Zoned residential land nationally: 7,911 hectares** (Goodbody 2024).
12. **Housing completions 2024: 30,330 units**, vs target of 50,500/year.
13. **Central Bank LTI limit: 4x income (FTB), 3.5x (SSB)**, 10% minimum deposit.
14. **Cost structure: 53% hard costs, 47% soft costs** (SCSI 2023).

## Key Findings from Analysis

15. **National weighted-average viability margin: approximately -57%** — development is deeply unviable at median land prices and median sale prices in most counties.
16. **No county achieves positive viability** at median RZLPA02 land prices with full buildcost construction costs and 17.5% profit margin.
17. **Dublin is the least unviable** at -22% margin, reflecting higher sale prices partially offsetting higher costs.
18. **The viability crisis is structural**: costs (construction + land + contributions + finance + profit) exceed achievable sale prices in every county at median land cost.
19. **Land cost is NOT the primary driver** — even at zero land cost, many counties would remain unviable because construction costs alone approach or exceed sale prices.
20. **Construction cost is the binding constraint** — at €2,690/sqm × 110sqm = €296k, construction alone is 76% of the national median sale price.

## Known Pitfalls

- RZLPA02 is an experimental series with limited transactions (355 nationally); small samples in many counties.
- Median land prices reflect the mix of transactions, not the marginal viable site.
- Sale prices vary enormously within counties (urban vs rural, new vs secondhand).
- The viability model is a simplified static calculation; real projects have dynamic costs and phased revenue.
- Part V impact is difficult to model precisely as the transfer mechanism varies by local authority.
- Construction costs vary by specification, site conditions, and economies of scale.
- Developer margin is not fixed; in tight markets developers may accept lower margins.

## Mechanisms Identified

- **Cost-price squeeze**: Construction costs rose ~70% since 2015, sale prices ~60% → compressing margins.
- **Land value stickiness**: Landowners maintain reservation prices above residual, preventing transactions.
- **Real options hold-out**: Irreversibility of development creates option value of waiting.
- **Credit ceiling**: Macroprudential LTI/LTV limits cap effective demand and achievable sale prices.
- **Infrastructure constraints**: Water, electricity, and transport connections limit serviceable land supply.
- **Labour market tightness**: Skilled trade shortages drive wage inflation above general inflation.
