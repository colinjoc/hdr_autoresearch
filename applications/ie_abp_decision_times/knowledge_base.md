# Knowledge base — ABP decision times

## Stylised facts (all sourced to primary ABP annual report appendices unless flagged)

- F01 **Headline decision time**. Mean weeks-to-dispose (all cases) by year: 2015 = 17, 2016 = 17, 2017 = 18, 2018 = 23, 2019 = 22, 2020 = 23, 2021 = 20, 2022 = 26, 2023 = 42, 2024 = 42.
- F02 **Pre-2018 compliance baseline**. SOP compliance historically at 70–80% (81% in 2011, 80% in 2013, 79% in 2014, 75% in 2015, 63% in 2016, 64% in 2017, source: Annual Report 2018 Fig. 3). Any analysis that treats 70% as a "normal regime" should cite this.
- F03 **2017–2018 step down**. SOP compliance dropped from 64% (2017) to 43% (2018). Plean-IT transition + Board vacancies pre-dating the 2022 crisis are the attributed causes in the 2018 Annual Report.
- F04 **2020 COVID effect was mixed**. In 2020 compliance *rose* to 73% despite an apparent backlog; the mechanical reason is that simple cases continued being decided while complex SHDs stalled, compressing the disposal mix to faster cases.
- F05 **2021 LRD transition**. 57% compliance in 2021; LRD legislation commenced in late 2021 but did not materially affect 2021 disposal mix since almost all 2021 disposals were SHD or normal appeals.
- F06 **2022 board crisis impact**. 45% compliance in 2022 — proximate cause is board-member vacancies following the Farrell Report (P057) and Hyde resignation.
- F07 **2023 trough**. 28% compliance — lowest on record since 1992 SOP inception. Press headline year (P053 Irish Times Oct 2023). Mean weeks to dispose = 42.
- F08 **2024 partial recovery**. Despite +43% FTE vs 2022 and -12% intake vs 2023, 2024 compliance was 25% — *lower* than 2023. Mean weeks (41.2 for normal planning appeals; 41.7 overall) essentially unchanged. Key interpretation: the on-hands stock cleared in 2024 included many accumulated 2021-2022 long-tail cases, so the *mix effect* offsets throughput gains.
- F09 **Case-type heterogeneity, 2024**. Mean weeks to dispose: Normal Planning Appeals = 41; Strategic Infrastructure = 53; Strategic Housing (ex pre-apps) = 124; All Other = 39. The 124-week SHD figure is a disposal-mix artefact: only the oldest SHD cases remained in 2024 for disposal.
- F10 **Q1 2025 recovery signal**. All-cases YTD SOP compliance = 41% by end of Q1 2025 (47% in March alone) — the first material improvement since 2020.
- F11 **Q3 2025 further recovery**. All-cases YTD compliance = ~65% (months Jan–Sep); formal disposals within SOP trending toward 70% in August-September.
- F12 **Capacity trajectory**. Total FTE: 2022=202, 2023=250, 2024=290 (+43% vs 2022). Board members: 2022=5, 2024=17. Hiring wave lagged the 2022 shock by roughly 12 months.
- F13 **Intake volume**. Total planning cases received: 2020=2,753; 2021=3,251; 2022=3,058; 2023=3,272; 2024=2,727. Peak was 2021 during the SHD tail.
- F14 **On-hands peak**. 3,616 cases in May 2023 (Q1 2025 report), including 648 RZLTs.
- F15 **JR lodgement rate for fast-track housing**. From companion project (PL-2): SHD ≈33%, LRD ≈36%, normal appeals ≈0.3%.

## Known pitfalls

- P1 **Mix effect confound**. Changes in mean weeks-to-dispose are partly driven by which case-types get disposed of in a given year. A compliance-trend analysis that does not condition on case type attributes throughput-mix changes to intrinsic slowdown. Every analysis must run case-type fixed-effects.
- P2 **SOP is an objective, not a deadline**. No case can be challenged for missing the SOP (P011 Simons; P034 McKillen). Analyses that treat SOP as a binding constraint overstate political pressure.
- P3 **Appendix annual tables are disposal-year**, not intake-year. A case received in 2019 and disposed in 2022 shows up under the 2022 Average Time Taken table as a 150-week case, not a 2019 case. Any panel analysis needs to choose a convention explicitly.
- P4 **"Formal disposals" vs "all disposals"**. Formal disposal = Board issues a decision. "Otherwise disposed" = withdrawn, invalid, or dismissed on procedural grounds. The all-disposal compliance rate is always higher than the formal-disposal compliance rate because "otherwise disposed" cases are quicker. Specify which one in every cell.
- P5 **Pre-application consultation cases are excluded** from Table 1 aggregates in most annual reports. Analyses that confuse pre-app-consultation volumes with appeal-intake volumes double-count.
- P6 **Q3 2025 data is partial-year**. Use YTD figures in analyses explicitly marked as interim. Do not extrapolate to full-year compliance without a stated extrapolation rule.
