# Knowledge Base: AARO Case Resolution Analysis

## Established Facts

1. **AARO cumulative total**: 1,652 reports as of October 24, 2024 [4].
2. **Resolution rate**: 292 cases resolved or pending closure (17.7% of total), all prosaic [4].
3. **100% prosaic resolution**: Every resolved AARO case identified as balloon, bird, UAS, satellite, or aircraft [4].
4. **Data insufficiency**: 444/757 FY2024 cases (58.7%) archived due to insufficient data [4].
5. **IC-merit cases**: 21 cases (2.8% of FY2024 intake) warrant further IC/S&T analysis [4].
6. **Blue Book base rate**: 94.4% identified over 12,618 cases (1947-1969); 5.6% unidentified [11].
7. **Hendry base rate**: 88.6% identified over 1,307 cases with personal investigation [8].
8. **GEIPAN base rate**: ~3.5% Type D (unidentified) after thorough investigation [133].
9. **NUFORC explained rate**: 0.54% (803/147,890) -- negligible due to no systematic investigation [38, 200].
10. **Backlog growing**: Intake (58.2/month) exceeds total resolution progress (22.5/month) by 2.6x [4].
11. **No evidence of ET**: AARO Historical Record Report found no evidence of extraterrestrial technology across 79 years of investigation [5].
12. **Circular reporting**: AARO attributes claims of reverse-engineering programs to circular reporting among believers [5].
13. **Starlink effect**: AARO increasingly resolves cases to Starlink constellation [4]; predecessor NUFORC project found measurable formation-report shift (p < 0.001) [200].
14. **Sensor artifacts**: Parallax, EO/IR compression, pixelation, and satellite flaring explain apparent anomalous performance [119, 4].
15. **FAA channel effect**: 392/757 FY2024 reports from FAA (51.8%), a new reporting channel [4].
16. **Geographic bias**: Reports cluster near military assets and sensors; collection bias, not stimulus [4, 66].
17. **No SIGINT/GEOINT/MASINT**: AARO received no reports through national technical collection during FY2024 [4].
18. **No health effects**: Zero adverse health reports during FY2024 [4].
19. **Nuclear UAS**: 18 reports near nuclear sites, all categorized as UAS by NNSA/NRC [4].

## Key Pitfalls

1. **Conflating "unresolved" with "anomalous"**: Most unresolved AARO cases lack data, not anomalous traits.
2. **Comparing AARO resolution rate to Blue Book**: Different eras, different data quality, different investigation depth.
3. **Treating NUFORC explanation rate as identification rate**: NUFORC has no systematic investigation.
4. **Cherry-picking the 21 IC-merit cases**: They are under investigation, not assessed as anomalous.
5. **Ignoring channel effects**: New FAA reporting channel alone explains much of FY2024 volume increase.
6. **Base-rate neglect**: Millions of aerial objects (planes, birds, balloons, satellites, drones) visible daily; even tiny misidentification rates produce hundreds of UAP reports.

## Limitations

1. **Aggregate data only**: No individual case records publicly available; analysis limited to report-level summaries.
2. **No breakdown of resolution categories**: FY2024 reports "balloons, birds, UAS, satellites, aircraft" but no per-category counts.
3. **Classified annex**: Additional information in classified version of reports; public analysis incomplete by design.
4. **Self-reporting**: All data depends on observers choosing to report; selection bias is inherent.
5. **No independent verification**: Cannot verify AARO's resolution assessments from public data.
