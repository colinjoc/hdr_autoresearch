## Verification of Phase 2.75 Mandated Experiments

### RV_BAYES_LIKELIHOOD -- RESOLVED
The Bayesian analysis now varies P(unresolved | anomalous) across {0.5, 0.7, 0.8, 0.9, 1.0}. The full 2D posterior surface is reported. The paper explicitly acknowledges that P(unresolved | anomalous) = 1.0 is an upper bound and that the 21 IC-merit cases suggest P(resolved-as-anomalous | anomalous) > 0. The posterior range at 5% prior is now reported as 4.3-8.2% rather than a point estimate of 8.2%. The assumption is documented and justified in Section 4.5. Row `RV_BAYES_LIKELIHOOD` present in results.tsv with status `RUN_RV`.

### CONTROL_BACKLOG_RATIO -- RESOLVED
The paper now reports both the adjusted ratio (1.7x, excluding catch-up) and unadjusted ratio (2.6x, including catch-up). The abstract uses 1.7x as the primary figure with 2.6x as secondary. Section 4.3 explains why the adjusted ratio is preferred. Section 5.2, Section 7 (conclusion), and the abstract all present both figures. Row `CONTROL_BACKLOG_RATIO` present in results.tsv with status `CONTROL`.

### DIAG_SELECTION_BIAS -- RESOLVED
A new Section 4.7 and Section 5.5 present the selection-bias diagnostic. The paper reports: 292/1,208 = 24.2% resolution rate among data-sufficient cases; 895 data-sufficient cases with unknown outcomes; the "100% prosaic" figure applies to 17.7% of total caseload. The selection-bias caveat now appears in the abstract, Section 2 (baseline), Section 5.1, Section 6.3 (new discussion subsection), and Section 7 (conclusion). Row `DIAG_SELECTION_BIAS` present in results.tsv with status `DIAG`.

## Verification of Non-Experiment Revisions

1. **Blue Book methodological criticism (Section 4.4)**: Added. Robertson Panel bias, Hynek's later dissent, Condon Report's 30% unexplained figure all cited. The 5% prior is contextualized within the 3.5-11.4% range.

2. **NUFORC "convergent patterns" reframed**: The abstract no longer uses "convergent patterns." Section 5.7 explicitly distinguishes trivially expected similarities (shape categories from perceptual constraints) from genuinely informative comparisons (Starlink as shared stimulus). The conclusion uses "shared perceptual patterns" rather than "convergent patterns."

3. **Resolution-rate range in abstract**: The abstract now reports "ranges from 7.1% (118/1,652 formal closures only) to 17.7% (292/1,652 including pending closure)."

4. **Selection-bias caveat on "100% prosaic"**: Applied in abstract ("subject to selection bias"), Section 2, Section 5.1, Section 6.3 (dedicated subsection), Figure 1 caption, and conclusion.

None. All five review issues have been addressed. The three blocking mandated experiments are present in results.tsv with appropriate status codes. The two non-blocking revision requests have been incorporated into the paper text. The paper accurately represents its findings with appropriate caveats.
