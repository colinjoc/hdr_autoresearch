# Knowledge Base — JR Tax on Housing Supply

## Established Facts

1. **SHD JR state-loss rate**: 14/16 = 87.5% in 2018-2021 (PL-1 E01-R1, verified by 27-test regression suite).
2. **ABP decision times**: rose from 18 weeks (2017) to 42 weeks (2023-2024) (PL-3 E00 baseline).
3. **SOP compliance collapsed**: from 69% (2019) to 25% (2024) (PL-3).
4. **Oaxaca-Blinder decomposition**: 100% of 2018-2024 rise is within-case-type productivity change, 0% mix shift (PL-3 R3).
5. **Utilisation rho**: peaked at 1.45 in 2022, recovered to 0.74 in 2024 (PL-3 E23).
6. **LRD JR comparison**: only n=2 decided LRD JRs by end-2024; comparison is below detection floor (PL-2 headline).
7. **SHD scheme sizes**: range from 100 to 741 units; median approximately 200 units; 8 of 22 cases have explicitly stated unit counts.
8. **ABP legal costs**: EUR 3.5M (2019) to EUR 8.2M (2020), per press reporting of ABP annual accounts.
9. **Construction cost inflation**: approximately 7% p.a. in Ireland 2018-2024 (SCSI indices).
10. **CSO completions**: 18,072 (2018) to 34,177 (2024), with plateau at ~20,500 in 2019-2021.

## Known Pitfalls

1. **Unit-count imputation**: 14 of 22 SHD JR cases lack stated unit counts in the OPR Appendix-2. Imputation from SHD minimum (100) or typical scheme size introduces uncertainty.
2. **Delay measurement**: JR delay is measured as lodgement-year to decision-year; actual delay includes remittal time (12-24 months additional for quashed cases) not captured here.
3. **Indirect channel attribution**: the share of ABP decision-time increase attributable to JR (vs capacity crisis, IT transition, case complexity) is not point-identified.
4. **Counterfactual assumption**: the 18-week SOP counterfactual assumes ABP throughput would have matched actual intake, which may overstate the gap if intake itself was endogenous to processing time.
5. **LRD-era exclusion**: excluding LRD-era JRs (n=2) understates the ongoing JR tax but is honest given the evidence base.
6. **Holding cost estimates**: EUR 500/unit/month is conservative (finance only); including opportunity cost (foregone rent) would approximately triple the figure.
7. **Completions vs permissions**: the counterfactual measures completions shifted forward in time, not permissions. The permission-to-completion lag (2-3 years) adds further uncertainty.
8. **2022 partial year**: the OPR Appendix-2 was published October 2022; 2022 case data is partial.

## Key Mechanisms

1. **Direct delay**: JR lodgement freezes scheme delivery; quashing requires re-application (12-24 months additional).
2. **Radiating effect**: JR-induced defensive drafting slows all cases, not just JR'd ones (Halliday 2004).
3. **Reason-giving expansion**: Connelly [2018] IESC 31 and Balz [2019] IESC 91 expanded duty to give reasons.
4. **Cost asymmetry**: one-way costs rule means filing JR is near-free; defending is expensive.
5. **Queueing dynamics**: rho > 1 causes exponential backlog growth; rho < 1 enables recovery.
6. **Construction cost inflation**: delay increases the real cost of delivery, potentially rendering some schemes unviable.
