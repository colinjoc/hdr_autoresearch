# Knowledge Base: Warp Drive Physics

## Established Facts

1. **The Alcubierre warp drive (1994) requires negative energy density everywhere in the bubble wall.** The energy density T^00 = -(1/8pi)(v_s^2 rho^2)/(4r_s^2)(df/dr_s)^2 is non-positive. This violates WEC, NEC, SEC, and DEC.

2. **Superluminal travel requires NEC violation.** Proved by Olum (1998) and Santiago-Schuster-Visser (2022). No loophole exists within classical GR.

3. **All Natario-class warp drives (unit lapse, flat spatial metric) violate the NEC.** Santiago et al. (2022) proved this as a no-go theorem. This class includes Alcubierre, Natario, and Lentz metrics.

4. **The Lentz (2021) positive-energy warp drive claim is incorrect.** Celmaster-Rubin (2025) demonstrated by direct computation that the Lentz metric has regions of negative Eulerian energy density. The errors were traced to: (a) the proposed potential is not a solution to Lentz's own PDE, (b) several derivation errors in the non-negativity argument.

5. **Subluminal positive-energy warp drives exist.** The Fell-Heisenberg (2024) solution satisfies all four energy conditions (NEC, WEC, SEC, DEC) for a subluminal constant-velocity warp bubble.

6. **The Fell-Heisenberg solution uses a matter shell with positive ADM mass (2.365 Jupiter masses) for a 10 m bubble at v=0.04c.**

7. **The Fell-Heisenberg solution cannot be extended to v > c.** As shift vector magnitude increases, momentum flux exceeds energy density, violating energy conditions.

8. **Any warp drive is a shell of matter moving inertially (Bobrick-Martire 2021).** This means warp drives require propulsion, just like any massive object. There is no self-propulsion mechanism.

9. **Superluminal warp drives create event horizons and enable closed timelike curves.** Proved by Clark-Hiscock-Larson (1999) and Shoshany (2023).

10. **The Casimir effect provides negative energy densities of order 10^{-3} J/m^3 at 1 micrometre separation.** This is approximately 10^{60} times too small for any macroscopic warp effect.

11. **Ford-Roman quantum inequalities constrain negative energy: |Delta E| * tau^4 <= C/(32 pi^2) in natural units.** This forces warp bubble walls to Planck thickness, requiring universe-mass energies.

12. **The Van Den Broeck modification (1999) reduces Alcubierre exotic matter to ~M_sun but is coordinate-equivalent to the original.**

13. **The Natario warp drive (2002) shows expansion/contraction is not fundamental to warp transport.** A divergence-free shift vector still produces warp travel.

14. **GWTC-3 (90 merger events) shows no anomalous gravitational wave signals consistent with exotic spacetimes.**

15. **NANOGrav 15-yr stochastic GW background is consistent with SMBH binaries, not exotic metric deformations.**

## Known Pitfalls

1. Confusing "locally subluminal" with "globally subluminal" -- in GR, effective superluminal travel is possible without locally exceeding c.
2. Treating the Alcubierre metric as a realistic engineering proposal -- it is a proof of concept within GR, not a blueprint.
3. Assuming the Casimir effect can scale to warp drive requirements -- the 10^{60} gap is fundamental, not engineering.
4. Conflating subluminal warp drives with conventional propulsion -- they are physically distinct (geodesic vs. non-geodesic transport) even if both are subluminal.
5. Assuming positive-energy claims automatically mean physical realisability -- the Fell-Heisenberg solution requires 2.365 Jupiter masses.
6. Treating energy condition violation as an absolute no-go -- quantum effects can violate energy conditions, but only by amounts bounded by QEIs.
