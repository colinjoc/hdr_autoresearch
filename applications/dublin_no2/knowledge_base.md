# Knowledge Base — Dublin/Cork NO2 Source Attribution

## Established Facts from Real Data

### WHO Guideline Exceedance
- **Every Dublin urban station exceeds the WHO 2021 annual NO2 guideline (10 µg/m³) in every year with data (2015-2023)**
- Winetavern Street (traffic) is the worst: 31-44 µg/m³ annual mean — 3-4x the WHO guideline
- Even suburban background stations (Ballyfermot, Tallaght) exceed WHO in most years
- **No Irish station exceeds the EU limit (40 µg/m³ annual)** except Winetavern in 2018-2019
- Rural stations (Lough Navar, Moate) are well below WHO: 1-5 µg/m³

### Source Contributions (validated against COVID lockdown)
- **Traffic is the dominant source at all urban stations**: 40-83% of total NO2
- At roadside stations (Winetavern, M50), traffic is 70-83% of total
- At background urban stations (Ballyfermot, Tallaght), traffic is 40-58%
- **Heating contributes 8-30% depending on station and season** (peak Dec-Feb)
- **Regional background is 8-25% depending on station** (higher % at cleaner stations)

### COVID Lockdown Natural Experiment
- March-June 2020 lockdown reduced Dublin NO2 by 28-62% vs same period 2019
- Largest drops at motorway/traffic stations: M50 Blanchardstown -62%, Davitt Road -52%
- Background stations also dropped substantially: Ballyfermot -53%, Clonskeagh -50%
- Even rural stations dropped: Lough Navar -56%, Moate -22%
- **The model's traffic attribution correlates 0.974 with actual COVID drops** (strong validation)

### Seasonal Patterns
- Winter (Dec-Feb) NO2 is 40-60% higher than summer (Jun-Aug) at urban stations
- Seasonal signal is a mix of: more heating, more congestion (school/dark evenings), worse dispersion (inversions)
- At background stations, winter excess is primarily heating
- At traffic stations, winter excess includes both heating and traffic congestion effects

### Day-of-Week Patterns
- Weekday NO2 at traffic stations: 26-28 µg/m³
- Weekend NO2 at traffic stations: 20-23 µg/m³ (20-30% lower)
- Tuesday-Friday are similar; Monday slightly lower than Tue-Fri
- Weekend reduction confirms traffic dominance (same heating, less traffic)

### Wind Direction Effects (Winetavern Street)
- NW/W winds give highest NO2 (40-42 µg/m³) — these bring city traffic emissions
- S/SE winds give lowest NO2 (28-31 µg/m³) — cleaner air from Irish Sea/rural south
- No clear port/shipping signal from NE/E (Dublin Port is NE of city centre)

### Dublin vs Cork
- Cork Old Station Road background station: similar patterns to Dublin background
- Cork South Link Road traffic station: 32 µg/m³ annual (comparable to Dublin traffic)
- Cork COVID drop: 36% — lower than Dublin, suggesting less traffic dependency or more heating

### Key Uncertainties
- Port/shipping contribution: not clearly separated from traffic in receptor data
- Heating fuel mix: cannot distinguish oil vs gas vs solid fuel from NO2 alone
- Construction/industry: lumped into residual
- Transboundary transport: included in rural background but not separately quantified
